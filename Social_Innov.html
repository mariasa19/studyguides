How the course is wired

The “Black Box” & the running question

The course doesn’t move through tidy ESG modules. It uses one recurring model and one recurring tension, and runs a case through them each session.

A social issueFramed as a global risk — societal, environmental, geopolitical, economic, or technological.
A caseA real firm facing a decision under that risk (Nestlé, Triodos, Danone, NEWSAN…).
A frameworkThe lens that explains how business is impacted by — and impacts — that force.
The testKarnani’s “Grand Illusion”: is this really a win-win, or a trade-off dressed up as one?

Karnani is the spine, not a single session

The “Doing Well by Doing Good: The Grand Illusion” trade-off diagram (Karnani) reappears in the Nestlé, Coca-Cola/Chiapas, and Triodos decks. Use it to interrogate every shared-value or “conscious business” claim in the exam: in efficient markets, private profit and public interest already align (no CSR needed); where markets fail, there is a genuine trade-off that goodwill alone won’t fix.
